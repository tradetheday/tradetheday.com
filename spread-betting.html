What is Spread Betting?

Understanding the basics of spread betting and how it differs from traditional trading.

A Flexible Form of Financial Trading

Spread betting is a derivative product that is tax-free (in permitted jurisdictions), which allows traders and investors to take advantage of price movements in stocks, Forex and other underlying financial assets.

It's also known as a flexible form of trading because traders can bet on bearish and bullish price movements, taking short and long positions accordingly. This form of trading is also popular because an investor's profit-making opportunity is higher in comparison to other formats.

üí° Key Insight: You can take a position on both increasing or declining price movements. If you think the price will rise, you "go long" and create a BUY position. If you think it will decline, you "go short" and open a SELL position.

The profit generation depends only on the accuracy of your forecast and the size of your opening position. For instance, if you create a short position and the price declines, your spread bet is profitable. However, if the price movement works against you, your spread bet will be a losing one.

What Is Spread?

The price you pay for opening a trade is known as spread. The spread is also known as the difference between the buy and sell prices.

These buy and sell orders are usually known as bid (sell) and ask (buy). This means that the trader will sell slightly lower than the middle price of the asset and will buy a bit higher than the middle price of the underlying asset.

What Are Risks With Spread Betting?

Understanding the risks involved before you start spread betting.

Important Risk Considerations

Like other forms of investment, there are a lot of risks associated with spread betting. Therefore, it's important to know about the risk factors before choosing a top spread betting broker and depositing real money.

Key risk factors include:

  • Leverage Risk: High leverage can amplify both profits and losses
  • Market Volatility: Rapid price movements can lead to significant losses
  • Account Close-out: Margin calls can force position closure at unfavorable prices
  • Market Gapping: Price gaps can bypass stop-loss orders

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Note: Trading financial derivative products, in particular spread betting products, involve high amounts of risk. If you don't properly use stop losses, your initial investment is at risk.

Spread betting is not suitable for traders that cannot afford to lose large sums of money. Always start with a demo account and never risk more than you can afford to lose.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Warning: Spread betting carries a high level of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. You could lose substantially more than your initial investment. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Only trade with money you can afford to lose.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is spread betting safe?

Spread betting is not safe. It comes with a lot of risks. If you don't properly adjust stop losses and take profit orders, you can lose money. You also need a lot of trading knowledge before spread betting.

How does spread betting work?

Traders are able to go short or long depending on market circumstances. If you think the price of an asset will go up, you can go long. On the other hand, if you think the price will move down, you can short the asset.

Can I use MetaTrader for spread betting?

Yes, hundreds of top-rated brokers are using MetaTrader and other software programs on their platform. Trading with a registered ECN broker is safe.

Is spread betting legal?

Yes, spread betting is legal in a majority of countries. However, it's illegal in the United States, among others. Always check your local regulations before starting.
